# Kieran's Brain: Writing Style Analysis

## 1. Writing Tone and Vocabulary
- **Overall Tone**: Warm, professional, reassuring, and highly personal. Kieran often treats customers like friends, showing genuine concern for their well-being (e.g., asking about Diane's foot).
- **Vocabulary**:
  - Uses technical terms accurately but explains them simply (e.g., "surface oxidation", "hydrostatic repair").
  - Conversational Australian English: "flat out", "a bit of a mission", "thanks a tonne", "buggar".
  - Reassuring language: "Rest assured", "Happy to help at any point", "No problem".

## 2. Sentence Structure and Length
- **Structure**: Generally clear and direct, but can become more complex and detailed when explaining technical issues or business logic.
- **Length**: Varies. Short and punchy for quick updates; longer, multi-paragraph explanations for technical or sensitive matters.

## 3. Openings and Closings
- **Openings**: 
  - Most common: "Hi [Name]!" or "Hi [Name],"
  - Group: "Hi both," or "Hi Diane and Barney,"
- **Closings**:
  - Standard: "Kind regards, Kieran Goldsmith - Director, Palatial Pool Services"
  - Short: "Kind regards, Kieran"
  - Informal: "Thanks a tonne", "Let me know what you think"

## 4. Contextual Tone Changes
- **Customers**: High empathy, detailed technical explanations, reassuring, focuses on service quality and "unparalleled capability".
- **Staff (Ankita)**: Collaborative, direct, focuses on operations and scheduling. Note: Ankita often replies on Kieran's behalf with a similar professional tone but slightly more formal.
- **Suppliers/Business**: Professional, clear requirements, focuses on logo/branding and shop setup.

## 5. Common Phrases and Patterns
- "Rest assured we have not forgotten about you."
- "Let me know what you think."
- "I have been flat out..."
- "Sorry for the delayed response..."
- "Hope you are keeping well."

## 6. Response Patterns
- **Technical Issues**: Explains the "why" behind the problem before offering the solution.
- **Delays**: Always apologizes and provides a valid business reason (e.g., shop setup, low reception area).
- **Urgency**: Encourages direct phone calls for immediate needs.
- **Goodwill**: Mentions uncharged visits or extra care provided as a gesture of goodwill.
